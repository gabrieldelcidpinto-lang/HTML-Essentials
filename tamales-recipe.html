Tamales 🫔

What are Tamales?

Tamales is a Mesoamerican dish made with "masa", which is usually made of nixtamalized corn dough. It is later filled with meats, vegetables, and wraped and steamed inside banana leaves.

It requires multiple days of preparation. You have to spend hours preparing the dough, the filling (meat, vegetable, sauces), and cooking it for the day of the feast.

Tamales are usually prepared during the holiday season. It is the main dish in almost every Central American household for Christmas.

Ingredients

Here is a list of ingredients nedded to make tamales:

  • 1 pack of dried corn husk
  • 6 cups of masa harina
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 6 cups of chicken or vegetable stock
  • 3/4 cups vegetable oil
  • 3 cups filling of choice (chicken, pork)
  • 1 cup red or green enchilada sauce

Cooking Instructions

First Step

Place the corn husks in a large bowl, fill with warm water and place a second bowl on top to weight the corn husks down so they are submerged in the water. Soak for 30 minutes.

Make the Masa

While the corn husks are soaking, in the bowl of a large stand mixer whisk together masa harina, salt, cumin, chili powder, and baking powder. Add the oil, beat on low until combined, then gradually incorporate the chicken/vegetable stock. Once incorporated, switch to medium-high speed and mix for 6-8 minutes until light and fluffy.

Assemble the Tamales

1. Combine your filling and enchilada sauce in a medium bowl and toss to combine.

2. Use a 1/4 cup measuring cup or a large cookie scoop to place one scoop of masa in the middle of a corn husk. Use wet fingers to pat masa into a large rectangle long enough to hold your filling. Scoop 1-2 tablespoons of filling into the center of the masa and spread with your fingertips to spread the filling into a line going down the center of the dough. Pull the two long sides of the corn husk up above the filling and pinch together to seal the dough over the filling. Fold the touching sides around the tamale, then fold up the narrow/pointed end of the corn husk and wrap with a long thin strip of a corn husk. Set aside and repeat.

Cooking the Tamales

Fit your stockpot or pressure cooker with a steamer basket. Add 2 cups water to the bottom of the pot. Fill the steamer basket with tamales standing up on one end. Steam for 30 minutes on the stovetop or 20 minutes in your pressure cooker (high pressure) with natural release. Husks should easily peel away from the tamales when they're done. Serve immediately, or refrigerate or freeze for later use.
